Hack Tennessee is not Hack Nashville

It's come to our attention that there are pockets of confusion in the community about Hack Tennessee. We want to be sure that you, a registered ticket holder, are well informed.

Although Hack Tennessee was created by two of the same founders as Hack Nashville, Hack Tennessee is not affiliated in any way with Hack Nashville.

Hack Nashville was operated by a group of six dedicated community organizers. Together, and with the support of many talented volunteers, it rose over three years and six events to become one of the most beloved events for makers in the southeast.

Unfortunately, because of differing visions for the future, Hack Nashville LLC ceased to exist on January 20, 2015.

It's possible that some of the other founders of Hack Nashville may create a new organization to continue on with their vision. If that becomes a reality, we hope you show them the same generosity you've shown us.

Avery Fisher and I are incredibly excited to continue to create a new platform where hackers from across the country can gather to create something amazing, stretch themselves to learn something new, and realize that Tennessee is a place that values makers.

Hack Tennessee is already on track to be the largest gathering of hackers ever in the southeast, with more than 400 registrations in the first week and 12,000 square feet of maker space.

If you have any questions, please don't hesitate to email me at brendan.wovchko@hacktennessee.com. We're excited to see you in May!
